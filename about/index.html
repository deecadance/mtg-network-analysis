About the Method

Archetypes first. Then synergy, uncertainty, and draft pressure.

The method presented in this website aims at constructing directional graph networks for MTG limited data. It tries to learn archetypes from decklists, then measures single-card strength, pair synergy, triplet motifs, and draft urgency inside each learned shell.

The formulas below are the ones the current implementation is built around. They are meant to explain what the website is doing, not to hide it behind generic language.

1. Archetypes from full decklists

Let \(X\) be the deck-by-card matrix built from the deck_* columns of the game export. Cards that appear in more than 55% of decks are filtered before clustering because they are too common to help separate one archetype from another. If a card shows up in almost every deck, it carries little information about which shell a deck actually belongs to.

\[ w(d,c) = (1 + \log x(d,c)) \cdot \log\!\left(\frac{1 + D}{1 + df(c)}\right) \]

This TF-IDF weighting downweights nearly universal cards and upweights cards that are concentrated in specific shells. The weighted matrix is reduced with truncated SVD and clustered with MiniBatch K-means.

2. Signposts

For archetype \(k\), let \(a_c\) be the number of decks in that archetype containing card \(c\), and let \(b_c\) be the number of decks outside the archetype containing the same card. The signpost statistic starts from the smoothed log-odds contrast

\[ \ell(c) = \log\!\left(\frac{a_c + \alpha_c}{N_k - a_c + \beta_c}\right) - \log\!\left(\frac{b_c + \alpha_c}{N_{\neg k} - b_c + \beta_c}\right). \]

Here \(\ell(c)\) measures how much more concentrated the card is inside the archetype than outside it. A larger positive value means the card is much more typical of that archetype than of the rest of the format.

The prior is centered on the card's global deck prevalence. In plain terms: before looking at a specific archetype, the method assumes the card should appear roughly as often as it does in the whole format. That prevents the statistic from overreacting when support is still limited.

\[ p_{0,c} = \frac{a_c + b_c}{D}, \qquad \alpha_c = 1 + 28\,p_{0,c}\,w_c, \qquad \beta_c = 1 + 28\,(1 - p_{0,c}). \]

The factor \(w_c\) slightly decreases the weight of rare and mythic cards. The reason is practical: a rare card can look archetype-defining simply because it appears infrequently and is strong on rate. Reducing its prior weight slightly makes the signpost ranking less eager to promote rarity alone as archetype structure.

\[ z(c) = \frac{\ell(c)}{\sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(\ell(c))}}, \qquad signpost\_score(c) = z(c)\sqrt{a_c}. \]

3. Deck-level pairs and triplets

After archetypes are learned, the method asks which cards co-occur in completed decklists more often than independence would predict.

\[ E_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b) = N_k\,\pi(a)\,\pi(b), \qquad L^{\mathrm{deck}}_{ab} = \log_2\!\left(\frac{O_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b)+1}{E_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b)+1}\right). \]
\[ E_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b,c) = N_k\,\pi(a)\,\pi(b)\,\pi(c), \qquad L^{\mathrm{deck}}_{abc} = \log_2\!\left(\frac{O_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b,c)+1}{E_{\mathrm{deck}}(a,b,c)+1}\right). \]

Once a strong deck triplet is found, the code feeds part of that information back into the pair graph. Concretely, if cards \(a,b,c\) form a strong triplet motif, then each of the three pairs \((a,b)\), \((a,c)\), and \((b,c)\) gets an additional bonus. This lets the network remember that a pair may matter because it sits inside a larger package rather than because the two cards are special in isolation.

4. Game-level pair synergy

Inside each archetype, the baseline is the archetype win rate \(p_k\). For a card \(a\), let \(p_a\) be its in-zone win rate. For a pair \((a,b)\), let \(p_{ab}\) be the win rate when both cards are present in the chosen game zone.

\[ standalone\_delta(a) = p_a - p_k. \]

The expected pair win rate is computed in logit space rather than raw probability space. The reason is that additive changes in log-odds behave much more cleanly than additive changes in bounded probabilities. If two cards each move win probability a little, the combined effect is closer to additive in log-odds than in raw percentage points.

\[ \operatorname{logit}(\hat p_{ab}) = \operatorname{logit}(p_a) + \operatorname{logit}(p_b) - \operatorname{logit}(p_k). \]
\[ synergy\_delta\_logit(a,b) = \operatorname{logit}(p_{ab}) - \operatorname{logit}(\hat p_{ab}). \]

A positive value means the pair wins more often together than its two single-card rates would predict inside the same archetype.

5. Standard deviation and confidence

The method estimates sampling noise with binomial variance and then propagates that uncertainty to the synergy statistic.

\[ \mathrm{Var}(p) = \frac{p(1-p)}{n}, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(\operatorname{logit}(p)) = \frac{1}{n\,p(1-p)}. \]
\[ SE_{ab} = \sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(\operatorname{logit}(p_{ab})) + \mathrm{Var}(\operatorname{logit}(\hat p_{ab}))}. \]
\[ \sigma_{ab} = \frac{|synergy\_delta\_logit(a,b)|}{SE_{ab}}. \]

The site shows confidence as a percentage because \(\sigma\) is easier to compare visually once it is mapped onto a 0-100 scale. The mapping used is

\[ confidence(a,b) = \operatorname{erf}\!\left(\frac{\sigma_{ab}}{\sqrt{2}}\right). \]

Here erf converts the signal-to-noise ratio into the corresponding Gaussian central probability. So a high confidence percentage means the estimated effect is large relative to its estimated sampling noise.

6. Pair quality, triplet quality, and draft corroboration

The graph needs one ranking number in order to decide which pairs should be emphasized. That number is built as a product. The product is used because the code wants a pair to stand out only when several conditions are true at the same time: the pair wins more than expected, it has enough support, it also co-occurs unusually often in decks, it may be part of a strong triplet, it shows up in projected drafts, and the estimated effect is not mostly noise.

\[ pair\_quality_{raw}(a,b) = \max(0, synergy\_delta\_logit(a,b)) \sqrt{n_{ab}} (0.02 + \max(0, p_{ab} - p_k)) (1 + 0.25\max(0, L^{\mathrm{deck}}_{ab})) (1 + 0.18\max(0, triplet\_bonus(a,b))) (1 + 0.05\log(1 + draft\_pair\_events(a,b))). \]
\[ pair\_quality(a,b) = pair\_quality_{raw}(a,b) \cdot \min(1, \sigma_{ab}). \]

This means that a pair with weak game lift, little support, no deck corroboration, or low confidence will be pushed down even if one ingredient is large. The product is there to make the score selective.

\[ triplet\_quality_{raw}(a,b,c) = synergy\_delta\_logit(a,b,c) \sqrt{n_{abc}} (1 + 0.20\max(0, L^{\mathrm{deck}}_{abc})) (1 + 0.06\log(1 + draft\_triplet\_events(a,b,c))). \]

7. Draft pressure, ALSA, and wheeling

ALSA is global, not archetype-specific. It measures what the whole draft table is doing.

\[ ALSA(c) = \frac{1}{N_c}\sum_i last\_seen_i(c) \]
\[ ALSA_{sd}(c) = \max\!\left(0.65, \sqrt{\frac{1}{N_c}\sum_i last\_seen_i(c)^2 - ALSA(c)^2}\right) \]
\[ \tau(c) = \max\!\left(1.1, 0.60\,ALSA(c) + 0.80\,ALSA_{sd}(c)\right) \]
\[ wheel\_model(c) = \frac{1}{4}\sum_{s=1}^{4} \exp\!\left(-\frac{\max(0, (s+8)-ALSA(c))}{\tau(c)}\right) \]

This is then combined with actual observed wheels through a smoothed posterior, so the website reports the probability that the card comes back when passed on picks 1-4.

8. Exported scores

Dependency score Weighted mean of positive incident synergy and triplet bonus. High means the card tends to need help from its neighbors.
Analysis score \[ analysis_{raw}(c) = signpost\_score(c) + 18\,dependency(c) + 140\max(0, standalone\_delta(c)) \] Then transformed, clipped, and rescaled to 0-10 inside the archetype.
Pick priority score \[ pick\_priority_{raw}(c) = 100(\max(0,draft\_win\_lift(c)) + 0.55\max(0,standalone\_delta(c))) \sqrt{\max(1,draft\_drafts(c))} (1 + 0.10\max(0, signpost\_lift(c)-1)) \]
Pick urgency Combines global market pressure with a smaller archetype win term. It is designed as a recommendation scale: 0 means easy pass, 10 means slam it.
Shell dependence \[ shell\_dependence_{raw}(c) = \frac{\max(0,support\_delta(c))\sqrt{\max(1,supported\_games(c))}(1 + 4\,dependency(c))(1 + 0.18\max(0,signpost\_lift(c)-1))}{1 + 16\max(0,standalone\_delta(c))} \]
Build-around detector A true build-around must clear all of these: supported games at least 80, support delta at least 1.8 points, dependency at least 0.075, and standalone delta at most 1.4 points.
